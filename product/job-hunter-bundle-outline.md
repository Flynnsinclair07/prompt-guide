## 1. Module breakdown

### Module 1 — Resume
- **8 prompts**: resume from scratch · resume rewrite · bullet rewrite · ATS keyword injection · quantify achievements · career-switcher angle · executive summary · LinkedIn-to-resume convert
- **ATS optimization workflow**: 5-step checklist (keyword density, section order, formatting, file type, scan test)
- **3 before/after worked examples**: entry-level · mid-career switcher · senior

### Module 2 — Cover Letter
- **6 prompts**: hook-first opener · mirror-the-JD · industry-switcher version · referral version · cold application · follow-up
- **3 templates**: 1-page classic · story-driven · brief-and-punchy
- **"What hiring managers actually skim for"** — 1-page cheat sheet

### Module 3 — Interview Prep
- **10 prompts**: behavioral Qs by role · technical Qs by industry · case interview · STAR answer structuring · weakness reframe · "why this company" research · closing questions to ask · salary screen · panel prep · post-interview thank-you
- **AI-grilling rehearsal workflow**: 3-round structure (warm-up · pressure · curveballs)
- **Answer-scoring prompt**: user pastes their answer, AI rates it, rewrites stronger

### Module 4 — LinkedIn Profile
- **5 prompts**: headline · about section · experience bullets · skills section keywording · engagement-seed posts
- **Optimization checklist**: 14 items (URL, banner, photo, headline, etc.)
- **Weekly content ideas prompt pack**: 10 post types

### Module 5 — Salary Negotiation *(mostly net-new)*
- **6 prompts**: offer evaluation · market-rate research · counter-offer scripting · justify your ask · multiple-offer leverage · deferred-start negotiation
- **5 copy-paste scripts**:
  - Email: decline first offer politely + counter
  - Phone: 30-second opener for salary discussion
  - Rebuttal: "We don't have budget"
  - Rebuttal: "Is that your final answer?"
  - Accept: lock in final terms
- **Walkaway math worksheet**: benefit-equivalency calculator

**Total**: 35 prompts · 5 scripts · 4 workflows · 3 checklists · 3 worked examples · 1 worksheet

## 8. Pricing rationale — why $39

**Anchors**:
- 1-hour career coach: $150–400/hr
- Rezi / Teal premium: $15–30/mo
- Individual ChatGPT prompt packs on Gumroad: $9–19 (commodity tier — what we're escaping)
- Salary negotiation coaching call: $200–500

$39 sits above the commodity tier (signals quality) and below the $50 "pause and think" threshold for active job seekers (they're outcome-focused, not price-focused).

**Anchor language for listing**: "Less than 15 minutes with a resume coach."

**Format choice**: one-time > subscription. Gumroad A/B data on bundles shows one-time beats $/mo by ~2× for this buyer.

**Upsell path (v2, not v1)**:
- Tier 2 add-on: async offer-letter review — $79
- Tier 3: full Career OS (future product) — $89

**Refund**: 14-day full, no questions. Friction reducer. Refund rate on this product type typically <5%.

## Risks + open questions

- **Notion duplication UX**: if users don't duplicate, one-click copy still works in view-only mode. Test before ship.
- **Salary Negotiation module is highest-value but also highest writing load.** Carve 4+ hours specifically for it.
- **Cover design consistency**: reuse the pin generator pattern (Python/PIL) — same fonts, same palette, same spacing logic.
- **Anti-piracy**: don't worry about it for v1. Every Gumroad download is watermarked; pirates aren't the target market anyway.
