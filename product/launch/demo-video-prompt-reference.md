# Demo video — prompt quick-reference

**Use case**: For the 60-second Gumroad listing demo video. Paste these two prompts side-by-side during screen recording.

---

## Tab 1: Generic ChatGPT prompt (the "before")

Paste this into ChatGPT:

```
Improve this resume bullet: Led marketing for product launch.
```

**Expected ChatGPT output to highlight on screen** (typical generic response):
- Inflated metrics not in the input ("increased revenue by 35%", "drove 47% engagement", etc.)
- Banned clichés ("spearheaded", "cross-functional synergy", "leveraged", "innovative solutions", "results-driven")

---

## Tab 2: Bundle's bullet-rewrite prompt (the "after")

Paste this entire block into ChatGPT (Module 1, Prompt 3 from page 4 of the PDF):

```
You are a resume writer who has rewritten 10,000+ bullets across every seniority level from intern to C-suite. You know which bullets get read in the 6-second recruiter scan and which get skipped — the gap is almost always specificity: concrete verbs, real numbers, and the named business outcome.

Original bullet:
Led marketing for product launch.

Context (optional but helpful):
- My role: Senior Marketing Manager
- The team size I led / worked with: 6
- The business outcome the work enabled: launched a new B2B SaaS product in Q2 2025
- Any metrics I can reference: 1,200 trial signups in first 30 days, $180K ARR booked in first quarter

BEFORE rewriting, do these checks:
- If the original bullet is a placeholder phrase ("duties include", "responsible for [X]", "worked on [X]") with no specific action or outcome, flag it and ask me to add one specific thing I actually did. Rewriting generic phrasing produces generic output.
- If I left the "business outcome" input blank or gave a vague phrase ("improved efficiency", "helped the team", "supported initiatives"), pause and ask me 1–2 questions to surface what specifically changed because of my work. Without a named outcome, the rewrite anchors on the action and loses the impact.
- If I said "NONE" or left metrics blank, do not invent numbers. Proceed with outcome-language only, and in the output flag which of the 3 versions would be strongest with a number I could supply later.

Once the checks pass, generate 3 distinct versions ranging from tight (≤15 words) to detailed (≤25 words).

Constraints:
- Every version starts with a strong action verb
- Every version includes at least one number if the context allows
- Do not invent metrics I didn't provide
- No corporate jargon: no "synergized", "leveraged", "spearheaded", "drove value"
```

**Expected bundle output**:
- 3 versions at varying lengths
- Real numbers (1,200 / $180K) used, no invented metrics
- No banned phrases (no "leveraged", "spearheaded", "synergized")
- Concrete action verbs

## 60-second script (read this verbatim during recording)

```
Most ChatGPT resume prompts give you garbage. Watch what happens with a vague prompt.

[Hit Send on Tab 1, let output stream for 5 sec]

Cross-functional synergy. Innovative solutions. Spearheaded initiatives. None of that means anything. Hiring managers skim past it.

[Highlight 2-3 banned phrases in the output with cursor]

Now watch the bundle's bullet rewriter.

[Switch to Tab 2, paste and run]

It refuses to write until you give it specific numbers. Banned-phrase list kills the AI clichés. The output is something a recruiter actually reads — and uses your real data, not invented metrics.

[Let output stream, highlight 1-2 clean phrases]

44 prompts like this. Launches May 17 — snipprompts dot com for the launch-week deal.
```

Approximately 55 seconds when read at conversational pace. Pad/trim with pauses as needed.
