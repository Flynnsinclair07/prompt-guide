Best ChatGPT Prompt for Summarizing Articles

Get the key points from any article, paper, or document in seconds — without reading the whole thing.

The Prompt
Summarize the following article for me. I need to understand the key points quickly.

[PASTE THE FULL ARTICLE TEXT HERE]

Give me:
1. A one-sentence TLDR (the single most important takeaway)
2. 5 bullet-point key takeaways
3. Any data, statistics, or numbers mentioned (listed separately)
4. The author's main argument or position
5. What's missing — anything the article doesn't address that it should
6. One question I should ask myself after reading this

Requirements:
- Write at a [8th grade / college / professional] reading level
- Keep the total summary under 200 words
- Don't add opinions — just report what the article says
- Flag if the article seems biased or one-sided

How to Use This Prompt

  1. Copy the full text of any article and paste it after the prompt
  2. For paywalled articles, use your browser's reader mode to grab the text
  3. Specify the reading level — "8th grade" gives simpler language, "professional" keeps technical terms
  4. For research papers, add: "Also explain the methodology and whether the conclusions are supported by the data"

Example Output

TLDR: Remote workers are 13% more productive than office workers, but companies are pushing return-to-office anyway because of real estate costs and management preference, not data.

Key Takeaways:

  • Stanford study tracked 16,000 workers over 9 months — remote workers completed 13% more tasks
  • 67% of Fortune 500 CEOs favor return-to-office despite productivity data
  • Companies with hybrid policies saw 25% lower attrition than full-RTO companies
  • The "collaboration" argument for offices is not supported by the meeting data — in-office workers spend 58% of their time in virtual meetings anyway
  • Younger workers (22-30) actually prefer 2-3 office days for mentorship and networking

What's missing: No discussion of industry differences — remote works for knowledge work but the article ignores manufacturing, healthcare, and service industries entirely.

Tips to Get Better Results

  • Batch summarize. Paste 3-4 articles and say "Summarize each separately, then tell me where they agree and disagree."
  • Academic papers. Add "Explain this like I'm not in this field" for papers outside your expertise.
  • Meeting notes. Paste raw meeting notes and say "Turn this into a structured summary with action items and owners."
  • Books. Paste a chapter and say "Summarize this chapter and tell me the 3 things worth remembering."
